BBeep: A Sonic Collision Avoidance System for Blind Travellers and Nearby Pedestrians
Seita Kayukawa, Keita Higuchi, João Guerreiro, Shigeo Morishima, Yoichi Sato, Kris Kitani, and Chieko Asakawa
CHI '19
We present an assistive suitcase system, BBeep, for supporting blind people when walking through crowded environments.

BBeep uses pre-emptive sound notifcations to help clear a path by alerting both the user and nearby pedestrians about the potential risk of collision.
Abstract
This work presents an adaptive video fast-forwarding technique that helps users browse long and untrimmed videos quickly. The proposed method is built upon the concept of the elastic timeline that a) allows users to select low-level cues of a video that they like to enhance and b) adaptively fast-forwards the video while keeping scenes with the selected cues played at a lower speed. The proposed method helps users to choose appropriate cues, in particular, a small set of object cues arranged automatically tailored to the contents of a given video out of many. Our user studies show that object cues selected by our method allow users to browse videos recorded under a variety of scenarios, such as cooking at home, strolling in the street, and playing in an amusement park, more quickly compared to the state-of-the-art interface with the elastic timeline in event finding tasks.
